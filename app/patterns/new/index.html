Scarf

Overview

Probably the easiest knitting project possible, while still producing a practical, gorgeous wardrobe piece!

This will be a simple rectangle -- you just need to choose a length and width. As always, knitgit patterns assume the same stitch is used throughout (no rib trim or single cables anywhere). Maybe you'll want to add tassles or fringe at the end, but again - the pattern is just going to calculate the basic rectangle for you. Feel free to riff on it from there!

With a scarf, it's important to choose a stitch that looks good on BOTH SIDES. Both sides don't need to be identical, but many pattern stiches have a good and bad side. Make sure you'll be happy wearing the scarf with both sides of the stitching visible.

With a scarf you also want to be wary of choosing a stitch that doesn't like to lay flat. Unless you deliberately want the scarf to coil upon itself like a tube, it's actually not a great idea to use a straight stockinette stitch.

Some options to consider: rib, moss, or - for retro 80s fabulousness - shaker stitch.
